Once upon a time, Swayamjit was born in the city of Kolkata. With years passing by, he has acquired a taste of avid learning, thinking, coding, investigating on research matters, reading, clicking pictures, writing blogs and listening to a wide spectrum of music.

Swayamjit is a Computer science engineering graduate from Swami Vivekananda Institute of Science & Technology college, Kolkata affiliated to West Bengal University of Technology. He has acquired research experience working under Prof. Garga Chatterjee as an undergraduate research assistant at the Ghilu : The Chatterjee Lab of Brain, Cognition & Society situated at Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata. He has also worked as a research intern under Prof. Kuntal Ghosh at the Center for Soft Computing Research: A National Facility (ISI Kolkata), University of Calcutta Technology Campus Salt Lake, Kolkata and Jadavpur University.

Feel free to reach out to him with queries relating to his work (or just say a 'hi'). You can download a copy of his CV here.

He likes to talk (a lot) and spends a lot of time doing so, often sometimes on random topics. He is self-critical about his shortcomings and feels correcting them will help him become a better person.

The pronunciation of his name in his native language, Bengali, is Swayam-jit (স্বয়ংজিৎ সাহা). It means one who can win by himself.

Research Experience

  • Research Intern, Center for Soft Computing Research: A National Facility (Winter 2019)

    The research work highlights a gap in the understanding of how the pandemic is impacting developing countries. It recognizes the effect of transportation in emerging economies where lockdowns can be ineffective due to the high population density.

  • Research Assistant , Indian Statistical Institute Kolkata (ISI) (Summer 2019)

    Worked at Ghilu: The Chatterjee Lab of Brain, Cognition and Society. People who have fairer skin tone as compared to the darker skin tone tries to dominate other in terms of public portrayal. These public portrayals are clearly seen in the case of visual mass media like cinema and advertisement. My paper explores whether such skin color tone based bias exists in case of Bangali ethnicity. The skin tone of heroes and heroines of popular Bangla films produced in West Bengal was taken as a proxy to explore the nature of skin colour tone based bias (if any), in case of the Bangla mother tongue population – the 5th largest mother tongue population in the world. First paper at http://dx.doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.3583051
